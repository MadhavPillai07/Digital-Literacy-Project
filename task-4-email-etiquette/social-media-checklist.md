# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible use checklist for students at VIT Bhopal University.

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and professional interests.
2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: would you be comfortable if your professor, future employer, or family saw this content?
3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts, location, and personal details on each platform.
4. **Give credit when sharing** – Always attribute content, images, or ideas that belong to others to avoid plagiarism and copyright issues.
5. **Engage positively** – Use social media to share knowledge, support peers, follow industry leaders, and participate in meaningful discussions.
6. **Report harmful content** – Use built-in reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, misinformation, or inappropriate content you encounter.
7. **Protect your personal data** – Avoid sharing your phone number, address, or Aadhaar details in public posts or with unknown accounts.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share OTPs or passwords** – No legitimate person or organization will ever ask you for these. Sharing them instantly puts your accounts and money at risk.
2. **Don't post confidential information** – Avoid sharing exam papers, internal notices, or sensitive college communications publicly.
3. **Don't engage with or spread misinformation** – Verify news before sharing, especially during exams, results season, or political events.
4. **Don't cyberbully or troll others** – Offensive comments, mocking peers, or posting embarrassing content about others can have serious legal consequences under the IT Act, 2000.
5. **Don't use social media as your primary news source without verification** – Cross-check headlines on official platforms or trusted news sites before forming or sharing opinions.
6. **Don't click on suspicious links in DMs** – Even if sent by a known contact (their account may be compromised), avoid clicking unverified links.
7. **Don't post your daily schedule or real-time location** – Sharing "I'm at the library until 9 PM" publicly can be a safety risk.
