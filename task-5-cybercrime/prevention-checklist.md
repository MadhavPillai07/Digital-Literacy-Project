# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have compiled this checklist to help my peers recognize and avoid common online threats.

---

## 🔐 General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** – Create passwords with at least 12 characters combining uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across platforms.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** – Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA provides a second layer of protection.

3. **Verify before you trust** – If you receive a message, call, or email asking for personal information — even from a "known" number — independently verify by calling back on the official number.

4. **Do not click suspicious links** – Avoid clicking shortened URLs or links received via SMS/WhatsApp from unknown contacts. These often lead to phishing websites that steal your credentials.

5. **Keep your software and apps updated** – Updates often patch known security vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates on your phone and laptop.

6. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks** – Do not log into your bank, email, or college portal on public or café Wi-Fi without a VPN.

---

## 💰 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory)

7. **Understand the difference between "Send" and "Receive" on UPI** – You should NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to accept a payment, it is a scam — you will be *sending* money, not receiving it.

8. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** – No bank employee, customer care agent, or government official will ever ask for these. Anyone who does is a fraudster. Hang up or block immediately.

---

## 📢 Reporting – What to Do If You're Targeted

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – File a complaint online within your state
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** (available 24x7) – Report financial fraud immediately; early reporting increases the chance of freezing the fraudulent transaction
- **Bank:** Call your bank's helpline immediately to freeze your account if you've shared credentials
- **Police:** Visit your nearest police station and file an FIR if required
