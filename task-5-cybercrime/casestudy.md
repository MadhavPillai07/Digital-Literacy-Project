## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive credentials, resulting in unauthorized transfer of money from the victim's bank account.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

**Scenario:** A first-year college student, Rohan, posts in a college WhatsApp group that he is selling his old laptop.

1. **Contact:** An unknown number contacts Rohan, claiming to be an interested buyer.
2. **Trust-building:** The scammer is polite, asks relevant questions about the laptop, and quickly "agrees" to the price.
3. **Request to receive money:** The scammer says, "I'll send you ₹500 as a token advance via UPI right now."
4. **Fake UPI request:** Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a **collect request** (a request for money) on the UPI app — which looks similar to a payment notification.
5. **Deception:** Rohan, unfamiliar with the difference between incoming and outgoing UPI requests, enters his UPI PIN thinking he is accepting the payment.
6. **Money transferred:** ₹500 — or more if the scammer sent a higher collect request — is immediately debited from Rohan's account.
7. **Disappearance:** The scammer's number goes unreachable.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- First-year college students new to digital payments
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with UPI interfaces
- People selling goods on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or college groups
- Job seekers asked to "pay a deposit" for a fake work-from-home offer

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often ₹500–₹50,000 depending on the request)
- Bank accounts may be further compromised if credentials are leaked
- Emotional distress, especially for students with limited income
- Difficulty in recovering funds — UPI transactions are near-instant and hard to reverse without RBI intervention
